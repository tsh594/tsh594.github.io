Estate Planning FAQs

Every adult should have basic estate planning documents. Major life events like marriage, children, or property acquisition are key times to create or update your plan.

We recommend reviewing your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life changes like births, deaths, marriages, or significant financial changes.

A will takes effect after death, while a trust can manage assets during your lifetime and after death. Trusts typically avoid probate and offer more privacy.
